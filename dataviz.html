DATA AND VISUALIZATION

We created a survey and had students involved in IU's radio take it. Below, you'll see the results as well as an explanation and an infographic.



This dataset asks if the person is new to radio or has been around for a while.

The second dataset asks what difficulties the students encounter when trying to recruit new members and expand the organization.

The third dataset is asking what majors make up most of the radio community.


The fourth study is asking what school the person is getting their degree from.

The fifth dataset asks what other organizations and activities students in radio are part of.

The last dataset is asking how the person became aware of IU's radio station and becoming a part of it.

This infographic shows the breakdown of college majors across the nation. We can see where we should be targeting to reach a wide demographic as compared to is currently being reached.

WHAT DOES THIS TELL US?

This data tells us who is most likely to be interested in radio and which schools are successfully recruiting members. It also helps us identify which part of the college campus population needs to be targeted better. Additionally, we learn which current recruitment methods are working and which aren't giving results. Overall, we can see which demographics are groups are being reached and how that's happening (National Center for Education Statistics).
